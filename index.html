Hello!


I'm a computational scientist and passionate musician from Brigantine, New Jersey. I'm deeply interested in a wide variety of computational problems, including machine learning, molecular simulation, and statistical mechanics.


I recently finished my Ph.D in chemical and biomolecular engineering from the University of Notre Dame, advised by Dr. Edward Maginn. As a member of the Maginn group, I performed classical molecular simulations to predict thermodynamic and transport properties of aqueous uranyl ions, which are primary components of nuclear waste. In addition, I studied the aggregation behavior of a recently discovered class of materials, uranyl nanoclusters.


In my spare time, I enjoy making music, exercising, and eating falafel.
