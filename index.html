Hello!


I'm a scientist from Brigantine, New Jersey, specializing in mathematical modeling and machine learning.


Currently, I serve as a data scientist on the IT&S Data Engineering and DevOps team at RWJBarnabas Health. In this role, I develop and implement machine learning models to generate actionable insights across clinical and non-clinical domains. Prior to joining RWJBH, I was a researcher in Dr. Edwin Michael's group at USF's School of Public Health, where I developed models of infectious disease spread to forecast infectious disease trajectories. I completed my Ph.D. in chemical and biomolecular engineering from the University of Notre Dame under the mentorship of Dr. Edward Maginn.


Outside of my professional work, I enjoy making music and analyzing bitcoin.
